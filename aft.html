Attention Fine-Tuning (AFT)

Alexander Liss · Generative Conversational Dynamics (CS 6795, Georgia Tech)

What if the reward signal is already inside the model? Attention Fine-Tuning (AFT) is a post-training framework that treats conversational flow as a trajectory through attentional state space. It combines a History-Aware Cross-Attention (HACA) reward—derived from the decoder’s own cross-attention—with KL-regularized weighted maximum likelihood, improving history integration in T5-Large on MultiWOZ 2.1 without human preference labels or a separately trained reward model.

On Phase F evaluation, the primary AFT checkpoint improves cross-attention focus by +9.2% relative to an SFT baseline on task-oriented dialogue. The public artifact includes training and evaluation code, committed metrics, and Hugging Face weights for reproduction.
